3. Microtomes: Precision Sectioning for Consistent Diagnosis

3.1 What is a Microtome?

A microtome slices extremely thin tissue sections (3–5 microns) so pathologists can perform high-clarity microscopic analyses. In diagnostics, consistency of section thickness directly impacts diagnostic accuracy.

3.2 Types of Microtomes

  • Rotary Microtomes – the most common, suitable for routine histopathology
  • Fully Automatic Motorized Microtomes – ideal for high-volume labs
  • Sledge / Sliding Microtomes – used for large or hard specimens
  • Ultra-Microtomes – used for EM-level sectioning

3.3 ISTOS Product Strengths

  • High-stability blade holder for vibration-free operation
  • Automatic trimming and sectioning modes
  • Integrated safety lock for blade exposure
  • Zero-backlash micro-feed system

5. Tissue Processors: Automated Dehydration and Clearing

Tissue processors automate dehydration, clearing, and infiltration steps to prepare tissues for embedding. They are the backbone of any medium to high-volume histopathology lab.

5.1 Processor Types

  • Carousel Tissue Processors – cost-effective, batch processing
  • Vacuum Automatic Tissue Processors – rapid cycles, low reagent consumption
  • Continuous Flow Processors – used for very high-volume labs

5.2 ISTOS Advantages
