Why specimen storage needs more attention in histopathology

Surgical specimens may stay in your facility for days, weeks or even months, and they are often stored in formalin-filled containers. Without the right cabinets, this can lead to strong odours, uncontrolled fumes and cluttered corridors that worry clinicians and auditors alike. [file:32]

Good storage is not just about shelves. It is about controlled ventilation, easy cleaning, secure access and a clear chain of custody for every container. Done well, it protects staff, preserves medico-legal records and keeps the lab looking professional. ‚úÖ
